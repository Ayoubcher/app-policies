Notely — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-02
Developer: Ayoub · Contact: ayoubcherqa2020@gmail.com

Notely collects NO data. Everything stays on your device. The app has no internet access.

Data Collection

Notely does not collect, transmit, or share any data. Specifically:

  • No analytics or crash-reporting SDKs.
  • No ads or ad networks.
  • No user accounts, sign-in, or sign-up.
  • No third-party SDKs that transmit data of any kind.
  • No network/internet permission is requested by the app (verified in the app manifest).

Data Storage

Notely stores your notes locally on your device only, using an on-device database (Room/SQLite). This includes:

  • Your notes: title, body text, pinned status, and last-updated time.

None of this data ever leaves your device. There is no cloud sync, no backup to remote servers, and no server component at all. Because the app disables Android's automatic backup for its data (allowBackup="false"), your notes are not included in Android device-to-cloud backups either.

Your notes are deleted when you delete them in-app, or automatically when you uninstall the app (which removes all app data from your device).

Permissions

Notely requests no Android permissions. It has no camera, microphone, location, storage, contacts, or internet access. There is nothing to enumerate here beyond this statement — the app's manifest declares zero <uses-permission> entries.

On-Device Processing

Not applicable — Notely does not use camera, microphone, ML Kit, or any other processing pipeline that touches personal media. All app logic (writing, searching, pinning notes) runs entirely on-device with no data ever leaving the phone.

Children's Privacy

Notely is not directed at children under 13. Regardless of user age, the app collects no data from anyone, so there is no personal information collected from children or any other user.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the update will be posted at the same URL where this policy is hosted. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
