VolumeBoost: Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-02
Developer: Ayoub  ·  Contact: ayoubcherqa2020@gmail.com

VolumeBoost collects NO data. Everything stays on your device. The app has no internet access.

Data Collection

VolumeBoost collects nothing. There is:

  • No analytics or crash reporting
  • No advertising or ad SDKs
  • No user accounts, sign-in, or sign-up
  • No third-party SDKs that transmit data anywhere
  • No network access at all: the app has no INTERNET permission and cannot send or receive data over a network, by anyone, ever.

Data Storage

VolumeBoost does not save, log, or store anything of its own: no files, no preferences, no database. The app has no local storage layer (no DataStore, no SharedPreferences, no Room database).

The only "state" VolumeBoost touches is your device's own system volume levels (Media, Ring, Notification, Alarm, Call, System), which it reads and adjusts through Android's built-in AudioManager. This is the exact same system settings your physical volume buttons control, not data created or stored by VolumeBoost, and it is never transmitted anywhere. Closing or uninstalling the app removes every trace of it; there is nothing app-specific left behind.

Permissions

VolumeBoost's installed app requests the following permissions (verified against the built APK):

PermissionWhy it's needed
MODIFY_AUDIO_SETTINGS Lets the app read and change your device's stream volumes and attach the loudness-boost audio effect. This is a normal (install-time) permission; Android does not show a runtime prompt for it.
com.ayoub.volumeboost.DYNAMIC_RECEIVER_NOT_EXPORTED_PERMISSION Not requested by VolumeBoost: this is a permission Android itself auto-generates for every app that registers an internal, non-exported broadcast receiver (used here so VolumeBoost's UI can detect when you press the hardware volume buttons). It cannot be used by any other app and carries no user data.

VolumeBoost does not request RECORD_AUDIO (microphone) or any other dangerous/runtime permission. The app never records or listens to audio: its "Real Boost" feature uses Android's LoudnessEnhancer, an output audio effect that boosts the loudness of sound already being played by your device (e.g., music, media) before it reaches your speakers/headphones. It does not read, sample, or have access to your microphone or any input audio in any way.

On-Device Processing

The loudness boost (LoudnessEnhancer) runs entirely on-device, inside Android's audio pipeline, on audio your device is already playing. Nothing is uploaded, streamed out, or processed on any server: there is no server, because the app has no internet access.

Children's Privacy

VolumeBoost is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age, because it does not collect information at all.
