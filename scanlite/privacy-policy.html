ScanLite — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-02
Developer: Ayoub
Contact: ayoubcherqa2020@gmail.com

ScanLite itself collects no data — your scan history stays only on your device. ScanLite is ad-supported and shows ads served by Google AdMob. To do that, the app requests the INTERNET and ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE permissions, used solely for loading and displaying ads. AdMob, as a third-party service, may collect device identifiers, IP address, and coarse ad-interaction data as described below. Watching one rewarded ad unlocks Pro for 24 hours: no ads, plus extra QR style options.

Data Collection

ScanLite (the app code we wrote) collects no data. Specifically:

  • No analytics or crash-reporting SDKs
  • No accounts, sign-in, or user identifiers
  • No first-party servers — the app has no backend
  • Your scan history is never uploaded, synced, or shared with the developer

Google AdMob, the ad network embedded in the app to show ads, is a third-party service and collects data independently of the app in order to serve and measure ads. This can include device identifiers (such as the advertising ID), IP address, coarse location derived from IP, app version, and ad-interaction data (impressions, clicks). This data goes to Google, not to the developer of ScanLite. See Google's own disclosures for details:

  • policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites
  • support.google.com/admob/answer/6128543

Data Storage (all on-device only)

ScanLite stores the following, in a local database on your device only:

  • Your scan history — the raw text/value of each code you scan or generate (for example a URL, a Wi-Fi network's name and password, a contact card, an email, a phone number, an SMS, a location, a product barcode, or plain text), its code format, its type, and the time it was scanned or generated.

This history can contain sensitive content, most notably Wi-Fi passwords captured from Wi-Fi QR codes. It is stored only in the app's private on-device database, with allowBackup disabled, and is never uploaded or transmitted by ScanLite itself.

You control this history directly in the app:

  • Swipe a single entry away, or use its delete icon, to remove it.
  • Use Clear all to erase the entire history at once.
  • Uninstalling the app deletes all of it permanently.

ScanLite also temporarily writes a PNG image of a QR code you choose to share to a private app cache folder, solely so the Android share sheet can attach it to the app you pick (e.g. Messages, email). This file lives in the app's own sandboxed cache, is overwritten on each share, and is deleted when you clear the app's cache or uninstall the app. It is not uploaded by ScanLite itself: it only leaves the device if and when you personally choose a destination app in the share sheet.

Ads and Pro

ScanLite shows ads to keep the app free:

  • Banner and interstitial ads, served by Google AdMob, appear during normal use. Interstitials are capped — shown at most once every third qualifying scan, with a minimum time gap between them — so they never interrupt every scan.
  • Rewarded ads: watching one, in full, grants Pro for 24 hours, which removes all ads and unlocks additional QR code style options (color presets, larger export size). Pro is currently free (watch an ad to unlock it) — there is no account and no payment involved.
  • Ad serving currently uses Google's test ad units during development; production ad unit IDs will be swapped in before the app is generally available.

Permissions

PermissionWhy
CAMERATo scan QR codes and barcodes live through the camera viewfinder.
INTERNETRequired by Google AdMob to request and display ads. Not used by ScanLite's own code for any network activity.
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATERequired by Google AdMob to check connectivity before requesting ads.

Those are the only permissions ScanLite requests. There is no location, contacts, storage, or microphone permission.

On-Device Processing

All barcode/QR detection and decoding uses Google's ML Kit bundled (offline) barcode-scanning model. Every frame from the camera, and every image you pick to scan, is analyzed entirely on your device. No image, frame, or decoded value is ever uploaded to ML Kit, Google, or any other server by ScanLite.

Children's Privacy

ScanLite is not directed at children under 13. The app itself does not knowingly collect information from anyone, regardless of age. Because the app shows ads via Google AdMob, standard AdMob data collection (see "Data Collection" above) applies to all users; ScanLite does not configure child-directed ad treatment.
