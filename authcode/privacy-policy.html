AuthCode: Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-07-02
Developer: Ayoub · Contact: ayoubcherqa2020@gmail.com

AuthCode collects NO data. Everything stays on your device. The app has no internet access: it does not request the INTERNET permission, cannot make network requests, and does not communicate with any server (including ours).

Data Collection

AuthCode collects nothing. There is no analytics, no crash reporting, no ads, no accounts, no sign-in, and no third-party SDKs of any kind that transmit data. Nothing about you or your device is ever sent anywhere, because the app has no way to send anything anywhere.

Data Storage

AuthCode is a two-factor authentication (TOTP) code generator. The only data it holds is what you type in yourself:

  • Your 2FA accounts: the issuer name, an optional label, and the secret key you enter (either typed or pasted) for each account you add, plus the code settings (digit count, refresh period) for that account.

This data is stored only on your device, in the app's private, sandboxed storage area that other apps cannot access. It is used exclusively to compute your 6-digit codes locally on your device, using the standard TOTP algorithm (RFC 6238). It is never uploaded, synced, or transmitted anywhere, because AuthCode has no server and no network access to do so even if it wanted to.

AuthCode is also excluded from Android device backups (allowBackup="false"), so your 2FA secrets are never copied into a phone backup, cloud or local. This is deliberate: it keeps your secrets from ever leaving the device, even incidentally.

Important trade-off: because AuthCode does not sync or back up your data by design, losing, resetting, or replacing your device means losing your saved 2FA codes, since there is no cloud copy to restore from. We recommend you also keep the backup/recovery codes your account issuers (Google, GitHub, etc.) give you when you first set up 2FA, and store those somewhere safe, in case you ever lose access to this device.

All app data is permanently deleted when you delete an account inside the app, or when you uninstall AuthCode.

Permissions

AuthCode does not declare any Android permissions. It does not request camera, storage, network, contacts, location, or any other permission: it does not need them, since accounts are entered manually and everything runs locally.

On-Device Processing

All TOTP code generation happens entirely on your device using local computation (HMAC-SHA1 per RFC 6238). No data ever leaves your device for processing.

Children's Privacy

AuthCode is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, of any age; it does not collect information at all.
